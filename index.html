My name is Django.

I have had 3 different names,

one for each foster home I was in.

I am a dog.

That means I act out my behaviors;

I can't tell you how I feel with words.

You will never know my history.

You can only guess where I came from,

so you'll have to take me as I am.

Raising a Rescue

Although there are so many animals in shelters, raising a 'rescue' animal isn't easy. One of the New Rattitude Rat Terrier foster parents recently wrote a great article relating the truth of many pet adoption stories. Janell Brown writes;

"I have this weird impossible little fantasy. It's a world where all the puppies of terrier breeds - in fact all working breeds of dogs - gave birth to hideously ugly puppies. Because then people who bought/adopted those puppies might actually think about what life with an active, intelligent dog will be like. Puppy blindness is so common and invariably the people most susceptible to it are the ones least able to actually provide what a dog needs be successful living with humans."

Adopt, Don't Shop

This is the catch-phrase that seems to be coming into vogue where pet procurement is concerned. Gone are the days of paying high prices for specific breeds. Thanks to social media, more and more people have heard about puppy mills, where pups are kept in inhumane conditions. And on the flip side, people are also made aware of pets in need. It is not uncommon for strays, ill and elderly pets to be transported into different countries to help find 'furever homes'. Having recently experienced a 'pet rescue', we are keen to share what we have learned, given the increased attention on the subject, because as cute as they are, rescuing a pup or older dog can be a challenge.
