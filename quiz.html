What your Rat Terrier wants YOU to know!

Rat Terriers are not overly furry dogs, and Ratty's from California and the southern US have even less hair. Consider this when you move them up to Canada or anywhere in the PNW. It's cold here, and wet and dark. For me, somebody who can't stand the culture of dressing pets, it was a big pill to swallow when I had to buy sweaters and a rain coat, but as Django wants to tell you - he gets COLD. And he does not like the rain and the way it coldly dribbles down his back and in his ears late at night. So, get over to Chilly sweaters and get shopping!

More Excellent Owner Info.

Dogs, especially rescue dogs and especially Rat Terrier rescue dogs can't tell you much. So with Django sitting here beside me I will translate as best I can, some things that Rat Terriers, especially rescued Rat terriers want you to know.Django has been with us for six weeks now and he is just starting to find his voice, so these are the things he would like you to know, and consider before you think about getting a Rat Terrier, and especially a rescue Ratty.

A rescued Rat terrier is just like a puppy, it will need to be taught, and taught kindly, with lots of positive reinforcement. Did you know that some dogs who have been caged, do not even know how to go up and down stairs? It is hard for us to imagine, but for them it is a reality, so you must have patience. You will need to teach them how to sit, come, stay etc. all through positive reinforcement. Never yell at a rescue, they will just shut and in confusion and hurt.

How Django came home.

After Buster left us last winter, we were just a couple of broken hearts wandering around.

Of course as time marches past you, you feel better and finally do stop crying. But even after the spring and summer have come and gone and you are happy and laughing again, there is still a place just big enough for a small dog to fit.

Enter Django. Or Bo Jangles as my dad calls him, or DejaVu as my mom calls him; or Niko, Stich or Rothko as he was known in his foster homes and at the shelter where he was picked up as a stray.

We were going to name him Vincent, as in van Gogh because he is missing a bit of ear....

But in the end we decided on Django, and then it was confirmed when we saw his little feet. He has 'high toes' and his front feet look just like Django Reinhardt's right hand....


And so it goes, four days have passed and I can't recall the last time we laughed from morning until night, every day. And I have never seen such a happy dog as this, when he jumps up on the bed at night where he goes smiling to sleep. Life is very, very good.

Food Dilemma!

So, were not vegatarian or vegan, but we try to do Meat Free Monday, so what happens to me when I discover that the best food for a RatTerrier is meat! Jimminy cricket - it's meat for breafast and meat for supper. And not just any meat, lamb, turkey, chicken, but I have yet to buy beef. I just can't make myself. It's a real ethical dilemma and I wonder what vegan dog owners do? It is something to ponder.

The Hard Parts

There's nothing good about goodbye's, I think that's a quote from Gilmour Girls. But regardless, it's true. Having to put Buster down was the hardest thing I have ever done, and I've done some really tough stuff. In my grief, I made an app that can hopefully help people take more of an active role in their pets illness and last days. It is a helpless feeling watching somebody slowly fade away, especially if you don't know if they are in pain or not. The app will be ready soon, I am making it with my awesome friend who is a vet.
